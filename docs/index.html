Acratos Decentralized Governance

In a world where big tech controls your information, Acratos Network returns power to the community. Here, you decide how the network you use every day evolves.

The Acratos Network Governance System

Unlike traditional platforms where decisions are made by executives in boardrooms, in Acratos Network you are an active part of every decision that shapes the network.

1. Participation with $ACR

Every $ACR holder has a voice and a vote. No barriers, no permissions. You decide the direction of the network, not a distant board of directors.

2. Technical Committees

Experts in different technical areas who review and propose protocol improvements, with technical veto power.

3. Governance Council

Elected representatives who oversee strategic direction and resolve disputes between different levels.

Take Control

How Digital Democracy Works

A transparent process where you have the real power. No hidden boards, no decisions made behind closed doors.

1. Initial Proposal

Any token holder can create a proposal in the governance forum, detailing the proposed change and its justification.

2. Community Discussion

The community discusses the proposal, suggests improvements, and debates the pros and cons during a defined period.

3-5 days

3. Temperature Check

Non-binding vote to measure community sentiment and gather feedback before proceeding with a formal on-chain proposal.

2-3 days

4. Formal Proposal

If the temperature check is positive, a formal on-chain proposal is submitted with all technical details and parameters needed for implementation.

1-2 days

5. On-Chain Voting

$ACR holders vote on the proposal with weights proportional to their stake. Minimum participation thresholds and qualified majority are required for approval.

5-7 days

6. Implementation

Approved proposals are implemented by the corresponding technical teams, with oversight from the Governance Council.

Security

Security Mechanisms

To ensure network integrity and security, we've implemented multiple layers of protection.

Lock-up Period

$ACR tokens used for voting are locked during a cooldown period after voting to prevent immediate selling and align long-term incentives.

Requires significant $ACR stake

Participation Threshold

$ACR holders can participate in network governance by proposing changes, voting on proposals, and delegating their votes to trusted representatives. The more tokens you hold, the greater your influence in decision-making.

Emergency Veto

A decentralized security committee can temporarily halt proposals that pose an imminent risk to the network, subject to thorough and transparent community review.

Periodic Audits

All code proposals undergo thorough audits by multiple independent parties before implementation on the main network.
